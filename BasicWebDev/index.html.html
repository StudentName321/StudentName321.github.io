As the World Wide Web continues to become more accessible, the scope of uses for websites keeps expanding. Business websites are built and maintained by both large, corporate chains and small, local shops. Social media websites allow us to stay connected with friends and family or share funny memes. News and entertainment write-ups have largely gone digital in lieu of their print predecessors. And of course, we even have websites that help us look for other websites! Using the internet is so integrated into many people's lives, that even some website names have solidified themselves in everyday conversation ("Google it").

The combination of faster internet speeds and emerging technologies and trends have made websites more efficient, engaging, and stylish. But no matter how advanced websites become, when stripped down, they are all structured with a language called HTML.
